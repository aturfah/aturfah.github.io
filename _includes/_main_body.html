About

Hello! My name is Ali Turfah, I am a PhD student in the Biostatistics Department at the University of Michigan. I am interested in the statistical and computational problems that arise in biological and healthcare data, such as models to handle high-dimensional data and the interpretation of "black box" models. Right now I'm working with Dr. Lili Zhao on problems related to vaccine safety and Diabetes.

I did my undergraduate studies in Data Science through the University of Michigan's College of Engineering, after which I worked for a year as a Data Scientist at Algo, a start-up in Troy, MI. I completed an M.A. in Statistics at Columbia University in December 2021, after which I worked in Dr. Chunhua Weng's research group on problems related to medical literature mining and evidence extraction.
